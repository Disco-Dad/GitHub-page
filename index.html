The "dark side of the Moon" refers to the hemisphere of the Moon that is not visible from Earth. This side is not actually dark; it receives sunlight just like the side we see. The reason we always see the same face of the Moon is because it is tidally locked with Earth, meaning its rotation period matches its orbit around our planet. The term "dark" is metaphorical, representing the unknown aspect of the Moon before space exploration provided images of its far side.
